Beyond Gene Lists — Clinical-Grade Variant Discovery

Whole exome sequencing (WES) targets the ~1–2% of the genome that encodes proteins yet harbours approximately 85% of disease-causing variants. At the VIB Nucleomics Core we have validated a complete end-to-end WES service on the Element Biosciences Aviti platform, pairing it with the Twist Exome v2.0.2 capture panel (37 Mb, 283,942 target regions) and a best-practice bioinformatics pipeline based on BWA-MEM2, DeepVariant, GLnexus, and SnpEff.

Our benchmark across 24 human exomes demonstrates uniform, high-depth coverage, outstanding capture efficiency, and a 100% variant quality-filter pass rate — meeting the standards required for both research and diagnostic-grade applications.

Key results at a glance:
  • Mean on-target coverage: 50.5× (range 40–51.5×)
  • 98.77% of genes with complete exon coverage
  • Target region dropout: only 0.31%
  • 28,856 high-quality on-target variants per cohort (24 samples)
  • 724 HIGH-impact variants — stop-gained, frameshift, splice-site
  • 100% variant quality-filter pass rate

How It Works

Our pipeline delivers much more than a VCF file. Comprehensive annotation links every variant to its gene, transcript, and predicted functional consequence. HIGH-impact variants are automatically prioritised for follow-up. Coverage reports document every gene and exon, clearly flagging the small fraction requiring targeted validation.

Beyond standard SNP and indel calling, the same WES data supports a range of advanced analyses:

  • Copy number variation (CNV) detection at exon resolution
  • Structural variants — deletions, duplications, inversions, translocations
  • Gene fusions and chromosomal rearrangements
  • Viral integration mapping (HPV, HBV, EBV, and others)
  • Mitochondrial variants and heteroplasmy quantification
  • Pathway enrichment, variant burden testing, and ACMG/AMP classification

What This Means for Your Research

The service scales from small pilot experiments to large population cohorts. Supported designs include genotype contrasts (wild-type vs. knockout), tissue mosaicism studies, case-control analyses, and family-based pedigree sequencing. Human and mouse exomes are routinely processed; other species are supported where validated capture panels are available.

All workflows are version-controlled, QC is reported at every stage with MultiQC dashboards, and a consultation service is available for experimental design, sample sizing, and interpretation of results.
